Jollof Rice

This is the Nigerian smokey jollof rice

Nigerian Jollof Rice or Jellof rice is a rich and incredibly tasty west African one-pot Meal. It is a very versatile dish, and it is usually made from scratch using Rice, Tomatoes, Pimento peppers, Tomato paste, Scotch bonnet, Onions, Salt, and other spices

Ingredients

Produce

  • Bay leaves
  • Garlic
  • Ginger
  • Onions
  • Plum Tomatoes
  • Red bell pepper
  • Scotch bonnet
  • Thyme
  • Large Tomatoes

Canned Goods

  • Chicken bouillon powder
  • Chicken stock
  • Tomato Paste

Pasta & Grains

  • Rice

Baking & Spices

  • Curry Powder
  • Salt
  • White pepper or black pepper

Oils & Vinegar

  • Vegetable oil

Dairy

  • Butter

Liquid

  • Water

Step By Step On How To Apply The Ingredients

  1. The Sauteening of the onions comes first. This should take about 3 to 5 minutes.
  2. Then Stir in the tomato paste and let it fry for about 5 minutes.
  3. Add the ginger and garlic and cook along with the tomato paste for another 2 minutes
  4. Add the blended peppers and leave it frying for maybe 15 to 20 minutes,there's no actually no particular time frame, it all depends on the quantity of water in your pepper, and the water has to be fully gone or dried up before continuing.
  5. Add the thyme, curry powder, salt, white pepper, and seasoning cubes. Adjust the seasoning at this point if there's need to.
  6. Once flavor is on point, then, stir in the rice. Stir until you cover each grain of rice with the sauce
  7. Now add the chicken stock. Give it a brief stir and cover it up with tight-fitting lid. You can use foil paper to cover it before using the lid if your lid isn't fitting enough. This is because jollof needs a lot of steam in order to turn out well.
  8. Once the rice comes to a boil, reduce the heat to medium immediately and continue to cook until the rice is done, about 20 to 30 minutes.
  9. Serve with fried chicken, spicy grilled chicken, grilled tilapia fish,or gizzards & plantain.
